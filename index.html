KUCCPS Application Guide

KUCCPS (Kenya Universities & Colleges Central Placement Service) places Form 4 leavers into government-sponsored programmes. Applications open February–April each year at kuccps.net

1
Await KCSE results from KNEC (released Jan/Feb)
2
Register at kuccps.net using your KCSE index number
3
Select up to 3 course choices in order of priority
4
Pay application fee of KES 2,057 via M-Pesa
5
Await placement letter — revision window opens for rejected applicants

Funding & Scholarships

Everything Kenyan students need to know about paying for university — from HELB loans to government bursaries and scholarships.

🎓

HELB – Higher Education Loans Board

HELB is Kenya's primary higher education funding body. It provides loans and bursaries to eligible Kenyan students joining accredited universities. Applications open annually in September. Visit helb.co.ke to apply.

1
Register at helb.co.ke with your national ID
2
Fill in the online loan application form
3
Upload: ID, admission letter, KRA PIN, parent/guardian details
4
Await review and approval (4–6 weeks)
5
Funds disbursed to university account and personal M-Pesa
6
Repay loan 2 years after graduation (salary deductions)
💵

📋
Eligibility Checklist
☑️ Must be a Kenyan citizen
☑️ Must have KCSE certificate
☑️ Must hold a valid admission letter from an accredited university
☑️ Must have KRA PIN (or parent's PIN)
☑️ Must not have previously benefited from HELB for the same level
☑️ Application must be submitted before the HELB deadline (usually September)
💡
Pro Tips for Maximum Funding
🏠

Apply for County Bursaries

Every Kenyan county allocates bursaries through the ward fund. Visit your county government offices or ward administrator in July–August to apply. Amounts range from KES 5,000 to 80,000.

📅

Don't Miss HELB Deadline

HELB applications typically close in September each year. Missing the deadline means waiting another full year. Register early at helb.co.ke as soon as you receive your admission letter.

🎯

Stack Multiple Funding Sources

You can combine HELB loans + county bursary + church/mosque bursary + employer sponsorship. Many students fund their education through multiple sources simultaneously.

📞

HELB Contact & Support

Call HELB: 0800 724 352 (toll-free) or email info@helb.co.ke. The helpdesk is open Mon–Fri, 8am–5pm EAT. Visit their offices at Upper Hill, Nairobi if needed.
